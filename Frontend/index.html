The Problem: Air pollution represented as a pressing global challenge

Air pollution is a pressing global challenge, harming human health, damaging ecosystems, and straining economies. Vulnerable groups—such as children, the elderly, and industrial-area residents—face the greatest risks. Although satellites like TEMPO and ground sensors provide valuable data, these sources are fragmented, not always accessible in real time, and rarely integrated into predictive tools. Environmental factors like weather, traffic, and local emissions make accurate forecasting even harder.

Heritage Impact

Protects monuments from harmful pollutants (SO₂, NO₂, PM2.5) that cause erosion, black crusts, and discoloration. Extends the lifespan of exposed monuments by 15–25% through proactive monitoring and alerts. Provides a practical tool for ministries and UNESCO to monitor and preserve landmarks like the Pyramids, Historic Cairo, and ancient mosques.

Health Impact

Saves lives: in Egypt, 1 in 6 deaths is linked to air pollution — our platform helps reduce this risk through early warnings. Lowers emergency hospital admissions (asthma, cardiovascular crises) by 10–15% with predictive alerts. Improves the quality of life for vulnerable groups, especially children and the elderly..

Environmental impact

Elevated ground-level ozone in tropical forests has resulted in a cumulative loss in carbon drawdown of 0.29 PgC per year since 2000, equating to approximately 17% of the contemporary annual land carbon sink in the tropics

This video shows the Executive Summary

Our project develops a web-based application that forecasts air quality by combining real-time TEMPO satellite data, ground measurements, and weather information. Current challenges include fragmented data sources and limited predictive tools, leaving vulnerable groups and decision-makers without timely alerts. Our solution leverages cloud computing and AI to generate short-term forecasts, deliver personalized notifications, and provide interactive dashboards for stakeholders such as schools, transportation authorities, and emergency responders. By turning Earth observation data into actionable insights, the platform empowers communities and policymakers to take proactive measures, protecting public health and contributing to cleaner, safer skies.

Who is Hach The Atom?

We are a multidisciplinary team of developers, researchers, and innovators passionate about using technology for social good. With expertise in web development, cloud computing, AI modeling, and public health, we transform complex Earth observation data into actionable insights. Our shared mission is to empower communities, policymakers, and vulnerable groups with tools that predict, prevent, and protect against the harmful impacts of air pollution—ultimately contributing to cleaner air, healthier lives, and more sustainable cities.
